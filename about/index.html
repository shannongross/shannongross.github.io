Hi, I'm Shannon

I use data science and computer modeling tools to support strategic decision-making surrounding water, sanitation, and public health. If you're interested in working on a project together, feel free to contact me or take a look at my online resume for a list of my professional qualifications.

Research Interests

  • I am devoted to preventing waterborne diseases, particularly for endemic countries where malaria, dysentery, and parasitic worms are a part of daily life.
  • In my academic research, I advocate for more strategic design of community health programmes which emphasize the importance of integrating external factors like infrastructure, climate, and behavior when tackling communicable diseases.
  • My work at HumTechLab over the past two years has been primarily aimed at bridging the gap between student researchers and humanitarian practitioners.

Recommended Reading

  • Nudge: Improving Decisions about Health, Wealth, and Happiness by Richard H. Thaler
  • Strategy: A History by Lawrence Freedman (A fascinating history of strategy used in military and business from Aristotle to Obama)
  • A Problem from Hell: America and the Age of Genocide by Samantha Power (American ambassador to the United Nations)
  • The World Is Flat: A Brief History of the Twenty-First Century by Thomas Friedman (Climate Change and Globalization)
